Typical College Diets Visualized

Data reference: USDA FoodData Central

Meet the Students

These 10 students represent the demographics of our survey (73% female, 27% male). Let's follow them through a typical day of eating.

Breakfast

Breakfast is often skipped or rushed. In this survey, 57% of students do not eat breakfast on a daily basis, and 33% of students eat less than 3 meals a day.

Lunch

Between classes, convenience takes over. 61% of students reported eating fried food 3-5 times a week, with 21% of students reporting eating fried foods daily. Notice how refined grains and low fruit intake start to show. 49% of students reported rarely eating fruit.

Snack

The afternoon slump brings snack-heavy, lower-quality choices for many, while a few opt for plant-forward options. 59% of students chose cheaper options over a healthier more nutritious foods.

Dinner

The final meal of the day. A chance to catch up on nutrition, but irregularity and low nutritional consumption persist.
